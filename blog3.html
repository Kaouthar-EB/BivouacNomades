What can you see in Lake Iriki Sahara?
In the Sahara of lake Iriki, you will discover the interesting old small village of Sidi Abd Anabi, and the old Ksar, which is the original living village of Elmhazil early tribe, the remaining construction is a sign of thousands of interesting stories in the old past. There is also the Marabou of the grand Holly father of the whole tribe of Elmhazil. This person who was a very important ritual person in the whole tribe at that time. People even now, still visit his grave to take the Baraka, to be healed from different illnesses and to solve different problems. For example, if a woman is seeking for marriage, but can’t find a husband or if she is married but, can’t be a pregnant, she visits his grave believing that he will help her. There are a lot of interesting old stories that people still believe in you might discover in visiting this nomadic small village. Superstition is part of the culture and beliefs here. The locals of Zaouit Sidi Abd Anabi survive as nomads although they do not move because they construct small houses out from the clay but they still survive on goats and dromedaries. They also have a little bit of agriculture. They grow some vegetables to support the family. You can also visit the nomadic school just few kilometers from the village of Zaouit Sidi Abd Anabi. This School was set up few years ago by a Spanish non-governmental association to help local nomad’s children to study to know how to read, how to calculate, and a little bit of French also.
Lake Iriki was considered one of the main biggest lakes in Africa. It was a house for many kinds of animals, such as gazelles, fennecs, a lot of kinds of birds as well as many kinds of fish. Life for nomads at that time was much easier because they find water for their animals in the lake and they survive on fishing and hunting in the lake too. My grandmother told me that Lake Iriki was called the stomach of the valley of Daraa that stretches from Ouarzazate and Zagora to Tagnit. Then, from there to lake Iriki to the sea of Tantan, but because of the strategy of Hassan 2, they constructed the dam in Ouarzazate . Consequently, no more water go through the valley of Ouad Daraa. Finally, the Lake was gradually drying until there is no drop of water in the deep surface. When the lake has just got dry, you can't pass nearby because of the bad smells of the dead animals and fish. Birds immigrated to other places and the ones cannot go far they sadly died one after the other.
